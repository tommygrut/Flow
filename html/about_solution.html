Problems
Have you ever seen an article that really caught your attention, but then you noticed the “+” sign in the corner of the picture and just scrolled past? This is something we have experienced ourselves and want to make a solution to give the people a better flow of information. Times are changing and so are the newspapers. We know that more and more people use the online newspaper rather than the old school paper issue.

According to an article from NRK.no [1] that are referring to statistics from SSB, online newspapers was more popular than paper newspaper for the first time in 2014 and are continuing to suffer a slow death. If you study the yearly survey Norway’s media barometer in 2017 [2] you can see that paper newspaper proportions are dropping every year, simultaneously as online newspapers increasing in total readers until 2017. This is where it gets interesting. In 2017 also the online newspaper reader proportion dropped from 56% to 52% which are quite a significant decline. We have a hypothesis that the barrier paid subscriptions make may cause this decrease, making our idea a gateway over this barrier.

A big problem with these barriers is how willing people are to pay for these features. With the amount of online newspaper existing today it would be comparatively expensive to subscribe to all your preferred newspapers online, in comparison to Flow. An example, if you are browsing a newspaper you only use from time to time and find an interesting article with subscription barrier, you will most likely just scroll by. This stops the flow of information and will make the people less informative.

Solution
The subscription based newspaper service “Flow” is our solution to the future of online newspaper and freedom of information. All the member of the group has worked together to make a prototype of the service that we think can take the barriers we have described. Flow is a online service currently only available on nett browser, but can easily fit in to an app interface. What we want to accomplish this service is simply gather as many online newspapers as possible and combine them into one superior newspaper. While prototyping this masterpiece our priority was to make it as accessible as possible. This is especially important for us, so we can reach out to every segment of the society. We want this product to be so user friendly that everyone can use it, whether you are blind, deaf or other challenges. In the finished product we will also have a working language selector, like the prototype is visualizing.
