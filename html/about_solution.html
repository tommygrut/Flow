Problem
Have you ever seen an article that really caught your attention, but then you noticed the “+” sign in the corner of the picture and just scrolled past? This is something we have experienced ourselves, and we wanted to make a solution to give readers a better flow of information. Times are changing and so are the newspapers. We know that more and more people use online newspapers rather than the regular paper issue.

According to an article from NRK.no, [1] referring to statistics from SSB(Statistisk Sentralbyrå), online newspapers were more popular than paper newspaper for the first time in 2014. And the yearly survey "Norway’s media barometer" in 2017 [2] shows that paper newspaper proportions are dropping every year. Simultaneously online newspapers are increasing in total readers. This is where it gets interesting. In 2017 the online newspaper reader proportion dropped from 56% to 52% which is quite a significant decline. We have a hypothesis that the barrier made by paid subscriptions may cause this decrease.

A big problem with these barriers is how willing people are to pay for these features. With the amount of online newspaper existing today it would be expensive to subscribe to all your preferred newspapers online. For example, if you are browsing a newspaper you only use from time to time and find an interesting article with subscription barrier, you will most likely just scroll by. This stops the flow of information and will make the reader less informed.
